Unsupervised 3D Part Decomposition via Leveraged Neural Radiance Fields

Jae Goo Cho1, Geonho Cha2, Hogun Kee1, Songwhai Oh1
1Seoul National University, 2Clova AI, NAVER

Our method unsupervisedly infers the 3D parts of articulated objects from monocular videos, leveraging NeRF models.

Abstract

We propose a novel unsupervised method for motion-based 3D part decomposition of articulated objects with a single monocular video. In contrast to existing unsupervised methods relying on optical flow or tracking techniques, our approach addresses this problem without additional information by leveraging Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) techniques. For training NeRF on dynamic scenes which consist of articulated objects, we design a mapping function suitable for the movement of articulated objects. To effectively capture complex movements of articulated objects, we incorporate a 3D reconstruction loss using the sequence of 3D occupied voxel maps for the entire scene. Experimental results demonstrate that our method outperforms existing approaches in terms of 3D part decomposition for articulated objects. In addition, we demonstrate that our method effectively tackles the task which decomposes 3D parts of novel-posed articulated objects.
